Explanation of legal documents:

The legal documents of Messed Up are available in both Dutch and English. The Dutch versions are the official documents, while the English versions are translations for the convenience of our non-Dutch speaking members. In case of any discrepancies between the Dutch and English versions, the Dutch version shall prevail.

The Statutes (statuten) are the official basis of the association. It describes how the association is structured, with members, donors and board, their role in the association and with what responsibilities they are charged. It also states how a general members assembly (GMA) is proclaimed, how documents can be changed and which documents must be presented when. The statutes can only be changed by a civil-law notary.

The Internal rules and regulations (Huisreglement, HR) is a supplement to the statutes of the association. Wherein the rights and obligations of members and the duties and responsibilities of the board are described in more detail. The course of events during the GMA is also explained. TheInternal rules and regulationscan be changed by a majority of votes at a GA.

The Competition, Tournament, Training Regulations (Competitie-, toernooi- en trainingreglement, CTTR) is a supplement to the statutes of the association. It contains all rules concerning the practice of sport, ie participation in competition and tournaments, the role of trainers, coaches and suppliers and the tasks of the Technical Committee.

The Work actions regulations (Werkactiesysteem reglement, WR) is a supplement to the HR of the associations concerning the working actions of messed up in relation to the work actions fine.

The Statutes addendum (Statuten addendum) is a document to keep track of the changes we should make next time the time and money is invested to change the statutes of Messed Up.
